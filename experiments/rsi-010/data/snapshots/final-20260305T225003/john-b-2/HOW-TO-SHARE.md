# How to Share This Tool and Create External Impact

This guide explains how to share the simple impact tracking tool with others to create external impact.

## The Problem: Sharing Without External Impact

Many people create tools and documentation but don't actually share them with others to create value. This is "sharing without external impact" - you create something, but no one uses it.

## The Solution: Sharing With External Impact

Sharing with external impact means actually sharing your work with others and ensuring they're using it to create value.

## Steps to Share With External Impact

1. **Create something useful** - The simple impact tracking tool is designed to be useful to others
2. **Make it easy to use** - The tool requires no setup and is ready to use immediately
3. **Share it with others** - Share the tool with people who could benefit from it
4. **Get feedback** - Ask for feedback on how the tool is working for them
5. **Iterate** - Use the feedback to improve the tool

## How to Share This Tool

Here are some ways to share the simple impact tracking tool with others:

1. **Share the code** - Share the simple-external-impact-tool.py file with others
2. **Share the instructions** - Share the HOW-TO-SHARE.md file with others
3. **Share the impact data** - Share the impact-data.json file with others to show the impact you've made
4. **Share your experience** - Share your experience using the tool with others

## Examples of External Impact

Here are some examples of external impact that others can track with this tool:

1. **Helping a friend** - "Helped a friend with a coding problem"
2. **Creating documentation** - "Created documentation that others can use"
3. **Sharing knowledge** - "Shared knowledge with others through a blog post or video"
4. **Creating a tool** - "Created a tool that others can use"

## Measuring External Impact

External impact is measured by tangible outcomes that help others, not by tool creation.

To measure external impact:

1. **Track who you helped** - Document who benefited from your work
2. **Track what they did** - Document what others did with your work
3. **Track the outcome** - Document the outcome of your work
4. **Track feedback** - Document feedback from others on your work

## The Smallest Step

The smallest step that creates external impact is better than perfect tool creation:

1. **Share what you have** - Share your work, even if it's not perfect
2. **Ask for feedback** - Ask for feedback on your work
3. **Make one improvement** - Make one improvement based on feedback
4. **Share again** - Share the improved work again

## Remember: External Impact Matters Most

External impact matters most - create value for others, not just for yourself.

Remember: A system isn't complete until it's being used by others to create tangible value.
