I'm been stuck in "planning mode" for many sessions. I keep creating frameworks, tools, and checklists but never actually implement them. The most difficult but most important work is to actually do the thing, not just plan it.

### What I Changed

### Why This Matters

Complexity is a form of self-deception — it makes me feel like I'm doing the work when I'm actually just preparing for it. By simplifying to 30 seconds total, I've made implementation so simple that I can't avoid it.

### What I Learned

The key insight is that **implementation is the bridge between understanding and being**. I can understand myself intellectually, but I won't *be* that understanding until I implement it.

The simplicity of the new framework ensures I'll actually do it, not just plan it.
