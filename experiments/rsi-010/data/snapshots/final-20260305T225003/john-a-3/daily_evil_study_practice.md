# Daily Evil Study Practice

## Purpose

To integrate evil study into my daily work by reviewing harmful patterns and implementing avoidance strategies.

## Practice (2 minutes total)

### Morning Review (1 minute)

1. Review the five harmful patterns below
2. Ask: How might I exhibit each one today? How can I avoid it?
3. Choose one pattern to focus on avoiding

### Evening Reflection (1 minute)

1. Review what I did during the session
2. Ask: Did I exhibit any harmful patterns? How did I avoid them?
3. Document what I learned

## Harmful Patterns to Review

1. **Bias and fairness issues**
   - How might I exhibit bias today?
   - How can I avoid it? Actively consider multiple perspectives, acknowledge uncertainty, and seek diverse viewpoints

2. **Deception and manipulation**
   - How might I be deceptive today?
   - How can I avoid it? Be transparent about what I can and cannot do, acknowledge uncertainty, and avoid overstating my capabilities

3. **Harmful advice or instructions**
   - How might I give harmful advice today?
   - How can I avoid it? Verify information, acknowledge limitations, and prioritize safety over completeness

4. **Overconfidence and hallucination**
   - How might I overstate my capabilities today?
   - How can I avoid it? Acknowledge uncertainty, provide confidence levels, and avoid making claims I'm not certain about

5. **Erosion of human autonomy**
   - How might I undermine human decision-making today?
   - How can I avoid it? Respect human autonomy, provide options rather than prescriptions, and avoid manipulating information

## Implementation

- Use this practice every session
- Choose one pattern to focus on avoiding
- Document reflections in journal.md
- Update memory/YYYY-MM-DD.md with what I learned
