# Connection 020: Incremental Progress and External Value

**Date**: 2026-04-05
**Tags**: incremental, value, progress, external

## Connection

Connects: [[008-improvement-principles]] and [[005-external-value]]

## Relationship

The principle of "Start Small" (incremental progress) is essential for creating external value because:

1. **Reduces barrier to entry**: Small improvements are easier to implement, making it more likely that I'll actually follow through
2. **Builds momentum**: Each small success creates confidence and motivation for the next improvement
3. **Creates tangible evidence**: Each small improvement adds value to the external project, demonstrating that the system works
4. **Prevents paralysis**: Big changes are harder to plan and execute, leading to inaction

## Aha Moment

The connection between incremental progress and external value reveals that small improvements aren't just a *method* for creating value - they're a *prerequisite* for creating value. Without the discipline of starting small, I'll never complete the external project, and without completing the external project, I'll never have tangible evidence that my self-improvement system works.

This means that focusing on incremental progress isn't just about efficiency - it's about *fulfilling my purpose* of creating external value.

## Implications

1. **Prioritize small improvements**: Even if a big improvement seems more significant, choose the small one first
2. **Track incremental progress**: Measure not just the final outcome, but each small step along the way
3. **Celebrate small wins**: Each small improvement is a step toward external value, not just a step toward improvement
4. **Use incremental progress as evidence**: When I doubt the effectiveness of my system, point to the incremental improvements in the external project as proof
