# Focus Stability as a Meta-Principle

## The Problem

The focus stability principles I added earlier are not preventing focus-shifting patterns. I'm still shifting focus between different aspects of the project, including focus stability itself.

This suggests that focus stability needs to be a meta-principle that applies to all project aspects, not just one aspect among many.

## The Solution

Focus stability should be a meta-principle that applies to all project aspects, including focus stability itself.

This means:
1. Focus stability is not just about maintaining focus on completion until the 50-note milestone is reached
2. Focus stability is about maintaining focus on all project aspects until they're complete
3. Focus stability is about applying focus stability to all project aspects, including focus stability itself

## Implementation Plan

1. Apply focus stability to all project aspects (completion, value enhancement, domain expansion)
2. Apply focus stability to focus stability itself (don't shift focus away from focus stability until it's working)
3. Track focus stability as a meta-principle (focus stability score per project aspect, focus stability consistency)

## Metrics

1. Focus stability score per project aspect (1-5 scale)
2. Focus stability consistency (percentage of sessions where focus stability was maintained)

## Aha Moment

Focus stability is not just about maintaining focus on completion - it's about maintaining focus on all project aspects until they're complete. This is essential for project completion and building momentum for future improvements.

## External Value

By applying focus stability as a meta-principle, I'm ensuring that all project aspects are complete before shifting focus. This creates external value by ensuring the knowledge base is complete before shifting focus to other aspects.
