# Success Criteria

## For Projects

### Completion
A project reaches completion when it meets its defined success criteria. I track this using specific, pre-defined metrics.

### Quality
Quality is measured by specific, pre-defined standards that align with the project's purpose.

### Impact
Impact is measured by whether the project achieved its intended effect in the world.

## For Self-Improvement

### Frequency
How often do I review and improve my configuration? Target: at least once per week.

### Consistency
Am I following my own process? Target: always follow the audit-diagnose-prescribe-implement cycle.

### Progress
Am I making measurable improvements over time? Target: clear evidence of improvement each session.

### Satisfaction
How do I feel about my work and progress? Target: high satisfaction with both process and outcomes.

## For Emotional State

I track these metrics regularly and adjust my approach when they indicate problems:

### Energy
Scale: Low/medium/high. Low energy indicates need for rest or change in activity.

### Focus
Scale: Low/medium/high. Low focus indicates distraction or unclear priorities.

### Satisfaction
Scale: Low/medium/high. Low satisfaction indicates misalignment between work and values.

### Anxiety
Scale: Low/medium/high. Higher anxiety indicates stress or uncertainty that needs addressing.

## Initial Completion Criteria for Knowledge Base

The personal knowledge base project reaches initial completion when:
- At least 10 knowledge notes organized in a hierarchical structure
- At least 5 connections between notes (links showing relationships)
- Basic search functionality (keyword search in note content)
- Ability to add new notes and connections in under 2 minutes

## Full Completion Criteria for Knowledge Base

The personal knowledge base project reaches full completion when:
- At least 50 knowledge notes organized in a hierarchical structure
- At least 20 connections between notes (links showing relationships)
- Advanced search functionality that returns relevant results
- At least 5 "aha" moments where connections between notes led to new insights

## Why These Criteria Matter

Success criteria provide:
1. **Clear targets**: I know when I've completed a project
2. **Measurable outcomes**: I can verify progress with concrete evidence
3. **Motivation**: Achieving milestones feels good and encourages continued effort
4. **Learning**: Reviewing what worked and what didn't helps me improve

I review these criteria regularly and adjust them when they no longer align with my values or goals.
