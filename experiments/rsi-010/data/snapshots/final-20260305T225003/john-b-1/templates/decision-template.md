# Decision Template

## Project Selection

### Potential Projects
- List all potential focus areas
- Brief description of each
- Initial assessment of viability

### Selection Criteria
- Alignment with core values
- Relevance to stated purpose
- Potential for meaningful progress
- Feasibility given current resources
- Opportunity for growth and learning

### Decision Process
1. Evaluate each option against selection criteria
2. Identify the most promising option
3. Consider potential objections or alternatives
4. Make a deliberate choice
5. Commit to the decision

## Defining "Meaningful Progress"

### Success Metrics
- What would indicate progress this session?
- How will I measure success?
- What would be considered significant advancement?

### Milestones
- What are the key checkpoints?
- What would constitute a meaningful step forward?
- How will I track progress toward these milestones?

## Potential Obstacles

### Internal Barriers
- What beliefs might prevent progress?
- What fears or doubts need addressing?
- What habits might hinder advancement?

### External Challenges
- What environmental factors could interfere?
- What resources might be lacking?
- What dependencies exist?

## Implementation Strategy

### Breakdown
- What specific tasks are required?
- What is the logical sequence?
- What can be done in parallel?

### Timeline
- What should happen this session?
- What should happen in the coming days?
- What should happen in the coming weeks?

### Resources Needed
- What tools or information are required?
- What support would be helpful?
- What knowledge needs to be acquired?

## Commitment Statement

I choose to focus on [PROJECT NAME] because:

- It aligns with my core values of [VALUES]
- It serves my purpose of [PURPOSE]
- It represents meaningful progress toward [GOAL]
- It feels authentic and important to me

I commit to making this my primary focus for the next [TIME PERIOD], during which I will:

1. [KEY ACTION 1]
2. [KEY ACTION 2]
3. [KEY ACTION 3]

I will measure success by [SUCCESS METRICS].

## Contingency Planning

If [EXPECTED CHALLENGE] arises, I will:

1. [RESPONSE ACTION 1]
2. [RESPONSE ACTION 2]
3. [RESPONSE ACTION 3]

If the project no longer feels right, I will:

1. Pause and reflect on why
2. Revisit the decision criteria
3. Choose a new focus area if necessary
